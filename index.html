In-Depth Project: AI & Embedded Systems

An in-depth analysis of a project integrating artificial intelligence, development boards, and Raspberry Pi for innovative edge solutions.

Project Overview

This project introduces a distributed safety system for road vehicles, where each car is equipped with a camera and a dedicated transmission module. The system is designed to proactively alert oncoming traffic about hazards previously encountered along the route. When a device detects a potential danger, such as a sudden obstacle, an accident, or a risky vehicle, it immediately broadcasts an open signal to all nearby vehicles equipped with the same system.

Vehicles traveling in the opposite lane receive and process the alert, notifying the driver through visual or audio warnings. This real-time information enables drivers to adapt their driving behavior to current safety conditions. The primary goal is to enhance road safety by providing early warnings about potential hazards in the relevant lane, thereby reducing the risk of accidents.

Technologies Used

  • ESP32 with LoRa module (x2): For long-range, low-power wireless communication between vehicles.
  • ESP32 POE: For network connectivity and power-over-Ethernet integration.
  • Raspberry Pi 5 8GB: For advanced data processing, camera management, and system coordination.

Key Features

  • Real-time hazard detection and broadcast to nearby vehicles.
  • Immediate driver notification via visual and audio alerts.
  • Distributed, vehicle-to-vehicle communication for increased coverage and reliability.
  • Edge processing for fast response and minimal latency.
  • Completely offline solution.

Impact

By leveraging edge AI and robust wireless communication, this solution aims to significantly improve road safety, providing drivers with timely, actionable information and reducing the likelihood of accidents caused by unexpected hazards.
